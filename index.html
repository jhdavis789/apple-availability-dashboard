Methodology

Data source: Apple's retail/pickup-message API, polled every 1-3 hours across 19 zip codes covering 12 US metro areas (~119 stores). Each poll returns per-store, per-product pickup availability as one of three states: available (in stock for pickup), unavailable (out of stock), or ineligible (not offered at this store).

Cycle detection: For each (store, product) pair, we build a timeline of availability state changes. A restock is a transition from unavailable/ineligible to available. A sellout is a transition from available back to unavailable/ineligible. One complete cycle = restock followed by sellout. Cycles shorter than 5 minutes are filtered as noise.

Glitch filtering: Snapshots where >80% of all (store, product) observations show "ineligible" are discarded as backend glitches (e.g., the Feb 9 outage where Apple's system briefly returned rank=0 for all stores).

Demand proxy: Apple does not expose inventory quantities through any API. Sellout velocity (time from restock to out-of-stock) serves as an indirect demand signal: faster sellouts indicate higher demand or lower stock allocation. Stores selling out within hours likely have both high foot traffic and limited inventory.

Limitations: Polling frequency (1-3 hours) means exact restock/sellout times have up to ~3 hour margin of error. Same-day cycles where both transitions happen between polls may be missed entirely. The "available" flag is binary (at least 1 unit in stock), so a store with 1 unit and a store with 50 units both appear identical until one sells out.
